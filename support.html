Getting Started

Installation

  1. Visit the Chrome Web Store and search for "XCred"
  2. Click "Add to Chrome"
  3. Confirm the installation when prompted
  4. Navigate to X/Twitter - XCred will automatically activate

How It Works

XCred analyzes publicly available account information on X/Twitter and displays a country flag with a color-coded border below each user's avatar. The flag shows the account's registered location, while the border color indicates the account's credibility tier based on multiple factors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do the border colors mean?

  • Blue Border - Government: Officially verified government accounts
  • Green Border - Trusted: High credibility score with verified information
  • Blue Border - High: Good credibility score
  • Purple Border - Moderate: Average credibility score
  • Yellow Border - Low: Below average credibility
  • Red Border - Suspicious: Multiple concerning indicators

What factors affect the credibility score?

  • Account verification status (Government, Business, or Blue checkmark)
  • Connection platform (iOS, Android, or Web)
  • Location consistency between account and app store
  • Account age
  • VPN or proxy usage
  • Number of username changes

Why don't I see a flag for some accounts?

Some accounts may not have location data available in X's public transparency information. XCred can only display data that X makes publicly available.

Is this extension tracking me?

No. XCred does not track your activity, collect your personal information, or access your login credentials. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Why is my timeline loading slower?

XCred makes API calls to fetch account information. To minimize impact:

  • Enable "Remote Sync" in settings to use shared cache
  • Data is cached locally to speed up subsequent loads
  • Rate limiting prevents excessive API calls

Troubleshooting

Flags not appearing

  1. Refresh the X/Twitter page
  2. Make sure you're logged into X/Twitter
  3. Check that the extension is enabled in Chrome's extension settings
  4. Try disabling and re-enabling the extension

Extension not working after update

  1. Go to chrome://extensions/
  2. Find XCred and click the refresh icon
  3. Reload any open X/Twitter tabs

Clear cached data

  1. Click the XCred icon in your browser toolbar
  2. Scroll down to find the cache management option
  3. Click to clear local cached data
